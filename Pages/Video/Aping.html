Short film — APING

I studied psychology at university, and one of the studies we were shown was by a man named Asch.

Asch was interested in conformity. Especially in how and why we do things that go against what we know is right or wrong. Without a doubt, it's an important topic. After all, the world is full of some pretty awful atrocities and the foot soldiers of those atrocities likely don't think what they're doing is correct or virtuous. If we understand why they still do them, we can help it.

So Asch put a bunch of people in a room and all but one of them were actors. He showed them a series of questions and, over time, the actors started to answer in obviously incorrect ways. The real person was always confused but they ended up following along anyway.

This is a film about rebelling against the system, by doing exactly what it tells you to do.

My role?

I co-wrote and directed this thing, and I was even one of the tall handsome dog-mask wearing scientists. It's funny, because you couldn't see my face.

Yes, the masks were sweaty and uncomfortable.

No, there was no part of me that enjoyed that.

I still have the masks.

But not because I like them, it's just pretty hard to offload leather dog masks without seeming even stranger than the guy you brought them from. Like, seriously...
